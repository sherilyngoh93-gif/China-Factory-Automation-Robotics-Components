Weichai Power and Sany Heavy China Summit takeaways: TAM expansion, global
ambitions, and margin discipline drive positive outlook
Both Weichai Power and Sany Heavy delivered incrementally bullish messages at
the May 20–22 Global China Summit, highlighting accelerating global ambitions,
margin uplift, and a focus on disciplined execution. Management teams from both
companies emphasized their ability to capture new growth opportunities in AIDC,
electrification, and international markets, while maintaining a clear focus on
profitability and shareholder returns.
• Weichai Power’s management leaned into the global AIDC power cycle,
reaffirming 2026 AIDC engine shipment guidance at 3,500–4,000 units,
with diesel engines as the mainstay and gas engines gaining traction for
data center applications. The company is targeting a 25–30% global AIDC
market share in the next 2–3 years, leveraging rapid capacity expansion, cost
leadership, and flexible production lines. Net margins for AIDC backup engines
are above 25%, with product performance now on par with global leaders.
Aftermarket and service are a strategic focus, with the company aiming to
capture 20–30% of lifecycle revenue, especially in North America. SOFC is also
a strategic growth area, with 30MW capacity targeted in 2026 and 200MW in
2027, and commercial-scale profitability expected after 100MW annual output.
Management highlighted that brand recognition and large-scale market
validation remain the next hurdles, but the company’s rapid capacity expansion
and cost structure are clear competitive advantages. In the HDT/machinery
market, Weichai’s engine share is recovering in 2026, with new energy
powertrain revenue targeted at Rmb6–8B, and market share gains accelerating in
large construction machinery engines, especially mining excavators.
• Sany Heavy’s management reinforced a consistent message of execution-
driven growth, with 2Q/2H momentum poised to strengthen as comps ease
and FX headwinds moderate. The company is prioritizing profit quality over
volume, with overseas share gains, pricing discipline, and cost-down as the core
growth algorithm. Sany is seeing broad-based overseas expansion, with Europe
and the Americas showing strong momentum and Q2 positioned as the highest
revenue and profit quarter of the year. Management emphasized a deliberate
refusal to engage in price wars, highlighting recent price actions and a focus on
margin durability. Industry consolidation is accelerating, with leaders like Sany
and XCMG able to sustain investments and defend pricing through the cycle.
Electrification is a tailwind and differentiator, but not yet the core driver, as
overseas penetration remains low. Sany’s internationalization strategy and
capital allocation are increasingly focused on balancing a high safety buffer with
lifting shareholder returns.

May HDT sales highlight resilient Y/Y growth, a sharp LNG pullback, and a policy-
driven surge in electric HDT penetration
May data confirm that China’s HDT market remains resilient, with Y/Y growth
holding up even as the seasonal sales peak passes. The most notable trend is a
sharp contraction in LNG HDT sales and penetration, as the oil-gas price gap
narrowed rapidly, while electric HDT penetration surged to a new high, driven by
both policy incentives and the shift away from LNG amid high gas prices. Export
demand continues to provide a solid underpinning, but the sustainability of current
electric HDT penetration will hinge on future subsidy policy.
