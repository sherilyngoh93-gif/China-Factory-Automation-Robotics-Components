share price moves reinforce our conviction in the sector’s leaders. Weichai Power
is our top pick, having delivered strong YTD gains (+121% H, +95% A) on the
back of robust earnings, margin expansion and its AIDC/data center engine story.
Sany Heavy and XCMG follow, both benefiting from global expansion,
electrification and mining exposure, with Sany’s shares up modestly YTD and
XCMG consolidating after a strong 2025. Hengli Hydraulic remains preferred for
its robotics and automation optionality, while Zhejiang Dingli remains our least
preferred pick, despite a recent rebound, given limited near-term catalysts and
ongoing volatility. The sector’s recent underperformance (with the exception of
Weichai Power) vs. global peers – Caterpillar (+57% YTD), Cummins (+33%
YTD) and the MSCI World Industrial Index (+12% YTD) – is not justified by
fundamentals, in our view. We expect renewed investor focus on operational
execution and structural growth drivers as policy clarity, pricing power and global
demand recovery play out through 2026.

HDT sales extend robust momentum YTD, with April marking another strong month for
the sector
• China’s HDT market continued to deliver impressive results in April 2026,
with monthly sales reaching 125k units, up 43% Y/Y. Cumulative HDT sales
for the first four months hit 434k units (+23% Y/Y), reflecting a strong
acceleration from last year and highlighting the combined impact of post-CNY
seasonality, ongoing fleet renewal and resilient export demand. The sector’s
ability to sustain this momentum into the second quarter underscores the
effectiveness of policy support and the underlying strength of replacement
cycles, even as the market navigates a more complex macro backdrop.
• LNG HDT penetration surged in April as favorable oil-gas price dynamics
continued to drive segment outperformance. LNG HDT sales nearly doubled
Y/Y, with segment penetration rising above 37%. The persistent oil-gas price
gap through most of April, driven by elevated oil prices and relatively stable gas
prices in China, further enhanced the cost advantage of LNG trucks and
supported robust replacement demand. However, the late-April uptick in LNG
prices started to erode this economic advantage, particularly in northern markets,
where diesel prices at private stations are now comparable. We expect this
narrowing price difference to weigh on LNG HDT sales in May, but we view
LNG HDT as a structural growth driver, supported by ongoing fleet
modernization and favorable policy tailwinds.
• Electric HDT sales maintained high Y/Y growth in April, but penetration
rates remained in line with the post-subsidy period (June-December 2025).
Electric HDT penetration ranged 25-30% in the first four months of 2026,
similar to the June-December 2025 period, when the China IV subsidy program
boosted e-HDT sales. The high Y/Y growth rate of over 60% in April was due
mainly to the low base, as the China IV subsidy kicked in only from June of last
year. Sequentially, the segment’s acceleration reflects both strong peak season
demand and the anticipation of new policy incentives, with further upside likely
as local implementation of the 2026 trade-in program rolls out in the coming
weeks.
• Export data for April confirm that global demand remains a key pillar of
strength for China’s HDT sector. Export HDT sales rose over 35% Y/Y,
supported by robust logistics and mining demand across Asia, Africa and Latin
America. While export volumes moderated slightly from March, the sector’s
diversified market access and competitive product offerings continue to
underpin healthy global growth, helping to offset any modest softness in the
domestic market.

Share price performance review
Share price performance across the China HDT and machinery sector remains
bifurcated despite robust operational momentum and upgraded outlooks, with
market sentiment still lagging the improving fundamentals. Our conviction in
Weichai Power, Sany Heavy, XCMG and Hengli Hydraulic as top picks is
reinforced by their strong execution, margin expansion and exposure to
electrification, mining and global infrastructure cycles. Weichai Power’s
AIDC/data center engine story and Sany’s global mining and electrification push
continue to set them apart, while XCMG and Hengli Hydraulic are well positioned
for the next leg of the upcycle, in our view. Zhejiang Dingli remains our least
preferred pick, given its limited near-term catalysts and recent volatility.
• Share price performance has been mixed across the sector YTD. Weichai
Power-H (+121%) and Weichai Power-A (+95%) have delivered strong gains,
reflecting robust earnings delivery, margin expansion and positive sentiment
around electrification and export growth. SANY Heavy-A and SANY Heavy-H
have posted modest gains of 3-4%, while XCMG and Zoomlion-A are down
5%. In contrast, global peers Caterpillar (+57%) and Cummins (+33%) have
outperformed, benefiting from defensive flows and margin resilience. The MSCI
World Industrial Index is up 12% YTD, highlighting the relative
underperformance of most China machinery names, which is largely attributable
to profit-taking, macro risk-off sentiment and investor caution around policy and
commodity price volatility.
