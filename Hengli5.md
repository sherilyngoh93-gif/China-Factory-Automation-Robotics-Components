Updating our model, 4Q25 miss likely a nuance
We update our model for Hengli Hydraulic after FY25 results, with the headline miss
mainly due to FX losses and inventory impairment, but our investment thesis remains
unchanged. Gross margin declined 1.3ppts, to 41.6%, and Op-CF contracted 27%
Y/Y, reflecting both non-recurring impacts and a less favorable product mix. Our
revised forecasts for FY26-28 are 7-10% above consensus, reflecting confidence in
recurring profit growth at a c.20% CAGR and new business upside not yet fully
priced in. The Mexico plant is progressing toward breakeven, with the turnaround
now expected in 2027, but this is not a drag on the group’s outlook. We remain OW,
seeing buying interest on the dip, and continue to view Hengli as the best-positioned
robotics supply chain play, with robust fundamentals and margin resilience still in
place.
4Q25 headline miss a nuance, with recurring profit and market share gains
underscoring the growth story
The FY25 results showed revenue of Rmb10.94B, up 16.5% Y/Y, slightly ahead of
market expectations, while net profit reached Rmb2.73B, up 9% Y/Y, but below our
pre-print estimate due to FX losses and higher inventory impairment. Gross margin
declined 1.3ppts, to 41.6%, and net margin fell 1.7ppts, to 25%. Recurring profit in
4Q25 was c.13% Y/Y, largely in line, with the miss driven by translation losses on
USD assets and provisions for the Mexico factory. Op-CF for FY25 decreased 27%
Y/Y due mainly to timing differences in bill discounting and increased cash
payments for salaries and materials, but underlying cash collection remains healthy.
Management confirmed that the fundamentals are intact and the machinery cycle
remains favorable, with market share gains across all key segments and margin
resilience supported by product mix optimization and operational efficiency.
Machinery cycle remains favorable, with robust domestic and export demand
supporting volume growth and market share gains across all key segments
Hengli’s core segment delivered solid growth in FY25, highlighted by strong
excavator demand. Excavator cylinder sales reached 625,000 units, up 23% Y/Y,
with large/medium/small excavators up 44%/26%/15% Y/Y. Pump and valve
revenues grew 23% Y/Y, with excavator valves and pumps up 26% and 20% Y/Y,
respectively. Non-excavator cylinders posted 5% growth Y/Y, with strong
contributions from new energy and marine, while shield tunneling cylinders are
expected to rebound sharply in 1Q26 after a decline in FY25. Overseas demand was
strong, especially in Europe and India, with large excavator cylinder exports up 24%
Y/Y and key customers like Caterpillar showing c.20% growth Y/Y. Hengli expects
stable overseas demand for the next one to two years and is expanding its footprint in
Africa and other regions.
Margin resilience remains core part of Hengli story, with product mix
optimization and operational efficiency helping offset OEM price pressure and
support profitability
Despite a decline in gross and net margins, Hengli’s margin profile remains robust,
supported by technical upgrades and product mix optimization. Pricing remained
stable despite OEM price pressure, and management confirmed that margin
resilience is driven by scale effects and ongoing cost controls. The company’s focus
on higher-value segments and its ability to execute on cost and product strategy amid
tariff uncertainties further validate the margin story. Management expects margin

Robotics and automation emerging as Hengli’s new growth engine, with
technical leadership and early-stage commercialization positioning company for
multi-year upside
Management emphasized that robotics-related revenue, while still modest, is ramping
up, with investments in equipment and R&D. The company is focused on linear
components, including ball screws and planetary roller screws, which are gaining
traction in advanced manufacturing and humanoid robotics. Sample deliveries for
bionic hand and humanoid robot projects are underway, and Hengli expects robotics-
related revenue to accelerate as commercialization advances. Technical leadership in
linear joints and ball screws is attracting global clients, positioning Hengli to benefit
from both the machinery upcycle and the next wave of automation.
FA/ball screw business scaling rapidly, with custom product sales and control
system collaborations expected to drive profitability and margin improvement
The linear motion business remains in early stages, with FY25 revenue exceeding
Rmb80M, but gross margin still negative due to market entry costs. Hengli targets
Rmb300M in revenue for FY26 and aims to achieve profitability next year,
supported by increased custom product sales and collaboration with control system
partners. The company’s R&D focus on micro ball screws, cost reduction through
lathe machining, and system-level integration positions it to capture incremental
demand as the robotics sector moves toward commercialization. Management
expects margin improvement as the business scales, with custom products
commanding premium pricing and partnerships with software control system
companies enhancing overall profitability.
Global expansion through Mexico and Indonesia factories progressing, with
breakeven in sight and flexible capacity planning supporting future growth
The Mexico factory is progressing toward breakeven, with monthly output expected
to reach Rmb100M in FY26, though a small loss is expected this year. Management
targets profitability in FY27 as annual revenue surpasses Rmb1B. North American
customer expansion continues, mainly through component exports, with plans to
localize raw material supply pending further feasibility studies. The Indonesia
factory contributed Rmb20M in revenue in FY25, with full ramp-up expected in
2H26 and annual revenue projected at Rmb200M-300M. Capacity expansion is
flexible, relying on equipment upgrades and process improvements, rather than new
plant construction. Fixed asset investment is at a low point, with future capex
focused on robotics and industrial pump/valve capacity expansion.
Management’s approach to working capital and cash collection signals
underlying business health despite headline Op-CF volatility
The briefing highlighted that the Y/Y decline in Op-CF for FY25 was due mainly to
timing differences in bill discounting and increased cash payments for salaries and
materials, rather than any deterioration in business fundamentals. Management
emphasized that receivables and collections remain healthy, and the cash flow
situation is expected to improve as new business ramps up. The shift toward more
bank acceptance bill settlements and less cash payments reflects evolving working
capital management, which should support future liquidity and operational
flexibility.

Segment-level insights reveal strong performance in motors and industrial
pump/valve, with targeted expansion plans for high-margin products
Management shared that the motor business achieved c.Rmb900M in revenue for
FY25, with gross margin recovering to 40-50%. The company plans to expand
capacity for travel motors, targeting motor segment’s total revenue scale to Rmb1B-
2B in the coming years. Industrial pump/valve products maintained stable growth of
30-35% annually, with broad applications in injection molding and forging. These
segments are expected to contribute increasingly to Hengli’s diversified revenue base
and margin profile, offsetting softness in other areas and supporting the overall
margin story.
Management highlighted that Hengli is expanding its footprint in Africa, with a
new subsidiary established in Guinea and plans to acquire land for local customer
service. The company expects overseas demand to remain stable for the next one to
two years, with strong growth in Europe and India. The regional strategy is designed
to capture incremental demand and mitigate risks from trade tensions, supporting
Hengli’s global expansion and market share gains across all key segments.

Investment Thesis
Hengli Hydraulic (Hengli) is a leading supplier of excavator hydraulic cylinders in
China, with a market share exceeding 40% and a strong position in the pumps and
valves market. The company is well positioned to leverage its leadership in the
domestic cylinder market, in our view, with opportunities for further market share
gains in segments with relatively low penetration, such as larger-sized excavator
cylinders, valves and pumps. Hengli has strategically prioritized business
diversification since 2022 to reduce its reliance on excavator products and supply
chain concentration within China. The company’s expansion into linear motion
products, particularly in the humanoid robot and machine tool segments, is expected
to contribute to revenue. This aligns with Hengli’s strategy to tap into high-growth
areas and capitalize on the increasing demand for precision components in advanced
manufacturing processes.
Valuation
Our DCF-based Jun-27 PT is Rmb121. The WACC rate used in our DCF analysis is
8.4%, with assumptions as follows: risk-free rate: 3.0%; market risk premium: 6.8%;
Beta: 1.0x (based on Bloomberg); after-tax cost of debt: 4.3%; terminal growth: 5%;
and target gearing: 25%.
Risks to Rating and Price Target

(2) slower-than-expected market share gains for pumps and valves; (3) a
deterioration in GP margin; and (4) escalating trade tensions and higher-than-
expected tariffs for China’s products.
