Our new PT of Rmb125 reflects margin expansion,
machinery upcycle, and optionality from robotics catalysts
Hengli’s 3Q25 results and recent management commentary have prompted us
to raise our FY25E earnings forecast by 7% and LSD for FY26-27E, with our
estimates now 4%/5%/8% above consensus for FY25-27E. The main driver for the
upgrade is margin expansion, which was a positive surprise in 3Q25 and is expected
to continue, supported by both product mix and operational efficiency. Gross margin
for 3Q25 reached 44.2%, up c.1.2ppts Y/Y, with core products such as excavator and
non-excavator cylinders maintaining GPM above 40%, and high-margin pump and
valve products performing even better. We are lifting our PT to Rmb125, the street’s
highest, from Rmb116, reflecting our increased conviction in Hengli’s growth
trajectory and the machinery upcycle, which is supported by robust domestic demand,
ongoing replacement cycles, and a clear recovery in export sales, especially in
Europe and North America.
While our base case assumes robust growth in core hydraulic and automation
segments, we see significant optionality from Hengli’s robotics initiatives,
including potential order wins for humanoid robots such as Tesla bot. Management
expects new business revenue (ball screw, linear motion, actuators, electric cylinders,
robotics) to reach Rmb100MM in FY25, stepping up to Rmb200-300MM by FY26
as capacity ramps and customer validation progresses. Longer term, management
believes the combined new business portfolio could contribute Rmb5–6B per year
within 3–5 years, assuming successful scaling and customer adoption. The market
has not yet priced in the full robotics upside, and we believe confirmation of major
orders could drive a step-change in sentiment and valuation, with robotics and
automation potentially contributing c.25%+ of group sales and gross profit by
FY28E.
Robotics and humanoid robot breakthroughs are the key
catalyst for a re-rating
While the stock has recently traded more in line with machinery peers, and
machinery fundamentals remain strong, we see the near-term breakthrough on the
humanoid robot side as the key catalyst for a re-rating. Hengli’s CIIF commentary
and recent briefings highlight the company’s confidence in its linear joint and ball
screw technology, which are essential for humanoid robots and advanced automation.
Management confirmed ongoing sample deliveries for bionic hand and humanoid
robot projects, and stated readiness to supply Tesla and other global leaders as
demand materializes. The market has not yet priced in the robotics upside, as
investors remain focused on the machinery cycle, but we believe confirmation of
major orders would drive a step-change in sentiment and valuation.
Industry context from Schaeffler, as shared at CIIF, reinforces the scale of the
robotics opportunity and technical barriers. Schaeffler’s management emphasized
that the humanoid robot industry is at an early stage, with technical, reliability, and
efficiency challenges still to be overcome before large-scale commercial adoption.
Key barriers include the need for high-load, high-output linear joints, advanced ball
screw and actuator solutions, and integration of control and hardware. Schaeffler
noted that the next wave of robotics will require breakthroughs in materials, heat
management, and control algorithms, with Chinese suppliers like Hengli well
positioned to benefit from domestic hardware leadership and rapid decision-making.

Scenario analysis: Bull, base, and bear case vs. our new PT
of Rmb125
The base case supports our new PT of Rmb125, anchored by margin expansion
and machinery upcycle.
In our base case, we expect Hengli’s revenue to reach Rmb10,855MM in FY25E,
Rmb15,185MM in FY27E, and Rmb17,702MM in FY28E, with gross margin
stabilizing at c.43%. This scenario assumes continued robust growth in hydraulic
cylinders, pumps, valves, and a steady ramp-up in new business segments such as
ball screws, linear motion, and actuators. New business revenue is projected to reach
Rmb2B by FY27E, with robotics and automation contributing c.15% of sales. EPS
CAGR is projected at c.20%, supported by margin resilience, scale effects, and
ongoing cost controls. Consensus upgrades are expected as the machinery cycle
remains strong, with China’s infrastructure and replacement demand driving volume,
and export recovery in Europe and North America providing additional tailwinds.
The bull case points to a PT of Rmb177, with the robotics upside mainly lifting
FY28E and beyond.
In the bull case, we assume Hengli secures major orders for humanoid robots,
including Tesla bot, with new business revenue (ball screw, linear motion, actuators,
electric cylinders, robotics) accelerating to Rmb3–4B per year by FY27E. While the
lifts in FY25-27E are more limited as robotics initiatives scale gradually, the real
step-change in growth and valuation comes from FY28E onwards, when robotics and
automation could contribute c.25% of sales and gross profit. Gross margin for these
segments is expected to scale to 30–40%, supporting further margin expansion at the
group level. This scenario would justify a PT of Rmb177, as the market re-rates
Hengli as a robotics leader and consensus earnings move 20–30% above current
forecasts. The bull case also assumes successful commercialization of new product
lines, strong customer adoption, and Hengli’s ability to leverage its R&D and
manufacturing scale to capture incremental demand in both domestic and
international markets.

More color on 3Q25 results was provided at the briefing,

Hengli delivered a strong set of 3Q25 results, with 31% Y/Y net profit growth and 25%
Y/Y revenue growth, both well above historical averages for an off-peak quarter.
Gross margin expanded by 1.2ppts Y/Y, supported by a favorable product mix and
operational efficiency gains. Revenue growth was particularly strong in key product
categories: excavator cylinders saw a 24% increase, non-excavator cylinders grew by
21.54%, and marine cylinders nearly doubled with a 99% surge. New energy
cylinders also performed impressively, rising over 70%, while other special cylinders
increased by c.36%. However, boring machine cylinders experienced a significant
decline of over 50%. In the pumps and valves segment, excavator valves and pumps
grew by 28% and 26% respectively, with non-excavator valves and pumps up 38%
and c.10% respectively.
Motor products remained stable, with aerial work platform motors up 15%, rotary
motors up 45%, and overall motors increasing by c.33%. Hydraulic systems and
castings also posted robust growth, up 50% and 27% respectively. These segments
are contributing to Hengli’s diversified revenue base and helping offset softness in
certain areas such as boring machine cylinders.

Machinery cycle, demand trends, and capacity expansion
are supporting Hengli’s growth outlook
Hengli’s 3Q25 performance is supported by robust domestic demand and a
continuous recovery in export sales. Domestic demand for hydraulic products has
shown strong growth, with sales rising by 22% in 9M25, driven by new equipment
needs and an ongoing replacement cycle. Export demand, while slightly lower due to
overseas pricing and differences in customer settlement locations, still reflects a clear
recovery trend, especially in regions like Europe and North America, where growth
rates are strong, and Southeast Asia which also shows promising prospects. By
product, Hengli mentioned that demand for small excavators is somewhat weaker
domestically, but medium and large excavators continue to perform well. GPM for
domestic sales reached 42% in 3Q25, while GPM for export products achieved
c.39%. Hengli noted that production scheduling for 4Q25 remains robust, indicating
sustained growth into 4Q25. The company is also seeing strong demand in industrial
applications, with new energy and marine cylinders leading growth.
Hengli provided more color on its capacity expansion plan, indicating it will
start new phase planning before current production is fully utilized. For example,
when the revenue from phase I capacity (such as Rmb2B scale) reaches a certain
threshold (c.Rmb1B), Hengli will start planning for phase II of capacity expansion
(targeting Rmb3-4B). This proactive approach is based on the expectation that phase
I may reach full capacity within 1-2 years, while the construction of phase II takes
over a year, plus an additional 6-12 months for recruiting and training staff. This
ensures that the new capacity can be brought online promptly as soon as phase I
reaches full production. The company’s localization efforts in Mexico and Brazil are
designed to mitigate tariff risks and support local market growth, with Mexico
already serving as a fully localized hub and Brazil’s factory site selection nearing
completion.

Investment Thesis, Valuation and Risks
Jiangsu Hengli Hydraulic Co Ltd - A (Overweight; Price Target:
Rmb125.00)
Investment Thesis
Hengli Hydraulic (Hengli) is a leading supplier of excavator hydraulic cylinders in
China, with a market share exceeding 40% and a strong position in the pumps and
valves market. The company is well positioned to leverage its leadership in the
domestic cylinder market, with opportunities for further market share gains in
segments with relatively low penetration, such as larger-sized excavator cylinders,
valves and pumps. Hengli has strategically prioritized business diversification since
2022 to reduce its reliance on excavator products and supply chain concentration
within China. The company’s expansion into linear motion products, particularly in
the humanoid robot and machine tool segments, is expected to contribute to revenue
from 2025 onward. This aligns with Hengli’s strategy to tap into high-growth areas
and capitalize on the increasing demand for precision components in advanced
manufacturing processes.
Valuation
Our DCF-based Dec-26 PT is Rmb125. The WACC rate used in our DCF analysis is
8.4%, with the assumptions as follows: risk-free rate: 3.0%; market risk premium:
6.8%; Beta: 1.0x (based on Bloomberg); after-tax cost of debt: 4.3%; terminal
growth: 5%; and target gearing: 25%.
Risks to Rating and Price Target
Key downside risks include: (1) lower-than-expected excavator sales volumes;
(2) slower-than-expected market share gains for pumps and valves; (3) a
deterioration in GP margin; and (4) escalating trade tensions and higher-than-
expected tariffs for China’s products.
